One of the most successful phones pre-smartphone, the Nokia 3310 was released in 2000 and became one of the best-selling for the Finnish company. Sturdy, reliable and with a long-lasting battery, 3310 came with the T9 writing technology, a 84x84 monochromatic display and few evergreen games, most notably, Snake 2. Nokia released a new 3310 in 2017, a revived version of the icon targeted as low-end.

Snake first appeared in 1997 on the Nokia 6110. It was programmed by Taneli Armanto, a design engineer in Nokia. The concept originated from the 1976 arcade game ‘Blockade’, developed and published by Gremlin.
